The Life of a Transaction

Four services, one story — how a purchase at your bank becomes a row in the ledger. Step numbers follow the sequence; each color is one arc of the story.

1–6 link a bank · once 7–11 sync · continuous 12–15 refresh · on demand 16–17 read · the ledger

No tokens in the browser

The browser meets Plaid exactly once — inside Plaid Link (step 3). Every data call happens on the backend, using an access_token that is Fernet-encrypted at rest and never returned.

Reprocess, never skip

Added, modified and removed all land in one database transaction, and the sync cursor is written strictly last (step 11) — a crash anywhere replays cleanly instead of losing data.

Safe to re-run

A per-item advisory lock (step 9) arbitrates webhook vs. cron; the nightly cron re-syncs everything; the refresh cooldown is claimed atomically (step 13) so retries and second tabs can’t double-bill.
